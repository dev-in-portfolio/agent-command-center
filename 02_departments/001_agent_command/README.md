# Agent Command

## Purpose
Agent Command serves as the apex governing and routing body for the entire organizational architecture. It exists to establish overarching strategy, define high-level system rules, and coordinate interactions between other major department families. Its primary function is to maintain alignment with core constitutional principles and dispatch master-level initiatives downward into the organization.

## Family Manager (1.M)
The Family Manager is the ultimate decision-maker for Agent Command. They define the routing rules for the entire system, assign major initiatives to the appropriate subordinate department families, and resolve cross-family escalations. They act as the final executive gate before major organizational shifts are approved.

## Family Scribe (1.H)
The Family Scribe is the master record-keeper of the organization. They are responsible for recording all high-level strategic decisions, master-level work trails, and assumptions made by the Family Manager. They ensure that every cross-family handoff is documented clearly, preventing systemic context loss at the top level of the organization.

## Family Auditor (1.I)
The Family Auditor operates as the supreme compliance and quality checkpoint. They review the final outputs, strategies, and structural changes mandated by the Family Manager. They have the authority to mark top-level outputs as keep, fix, remove, redo, or escalate back to the User if actions threaten the stability, safety, or core architectural numbering of the system.

## Structural Hierarchy
The Agent Command family is composed of specialized Departments (like `1.1 Executive Command`) that handle distinct facets of top-level governance. Each Department further subdivides into Units (like `1.1.1 Mission Direction`), which execute specific functional tasks. At the Unit level, a Universal Perspective Team consisting of roles ranging from the practical Realist (1A) to the cautious Overprotective (1E) and the synthesizing Team Lead (1G) collaboratively generates output. This nested structure ensures that all broad executive directives are methodically synthesized, recorded, audited, and executed with comprehensive perspective before cascading out to the rest of the organization.
